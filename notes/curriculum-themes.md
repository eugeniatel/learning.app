# Subject themes

This is a loose map, not a fixed syllabus. Weeks move with the reader: a subject advances when the current concept, protocol, or novel part has produced useful notes.

Benchmarks: see `notes/university-course-benchmarks.md` for the Stanford, MIT, and Yale course sources behind this structure.

## LLM and AI systems

Time budget: 2 hours per week.

Themes:

- Architecture: attention, residual stream, MLPs, tokenization, scaling, inference.
- Post-training: SFT, preference learning, RLVR, reasoning models.
- Interpretability: probing, logit lens, circuits, sparse autoencoders, steering.

Current focus: attention as the first architecture concept.

## DeFi and protocol risk

Time budget: 2 hours per week.

Themes:

- Stablecoins: collateral, redemption, peg defense, balance-sheet risk.
- Lending and liquidation: collateral path, oracle dependence, liquidation execution, keeper capacity.
- Protocol lenses: Morpho first, then Euler and Silo, using a reusable comparison frame.

Approved structure: DeFi is protocol-risk analysis, not an academic survey. Each lending protocol case study uses the same practitioner lens: primitive, market structure, risk parameters, oracle path, liquidation path, bad-debt path, external dependencies, stress scenario, and protocol comparison.

Current focus: Morpho as a lending-market primitive.

Queued next: Euler V2 as modular lending infrastructure, with emphasis on vault architecture, the Ethereum Vault Connector, risk curator discretion, oracle choice, and Dutch liquidation mechanics.

## Dr. Zhivago

Time budget: 2 hours per week.

Themes:

- One part of the novel at a time.
- Reading is assumed; app tasks should teach and orient after the reading.
- Historical context only where it enriches the current part.
- Literary perspective: private life, conscience, faith, love, revolution, and historical force.
- Every week includes one required article, paper, or essay as a secondary source.

Approved task types:

- Context brief: prepared explanation of the historical and social world.
- Reference notes: names, places, political terms, religious references, and literary cues.
- Character map: who appears, how they relate, and what social world they represent.
- Scene lens: one taught interpretation of a key scene or pattern.
- Secondary source: one article, paper, or essay each week.
- One question: a small note to carry into the next part.

Current focus: Part 1, The Five OClock Express. Required secondary source: Fedor Dvinyatin, "The October Revolution as the Passion of Christ: Boris Pasternak's Easter Narrative in Doctor Zhivago and Its Cultural Contexts."

Edition: Vintage Classics Russians Series paperback, translated by Richard Pevear and Larissa Volokhonsky, ISBN 9781784871925.
